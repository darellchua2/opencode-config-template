# §3 Introduction — LEFT ARM Template

> **Horseshoe role:** Promise what the paper will deliver. The Conclusion
> (§3.7) will mirror this section paragraph-by-paragraph.

## 5-Paragraph Structure

### ¶1 — Real-world problem

Open with a **concrete engineering / industry pain point** (cost, safety,
downtime, hazard, accuracy gap). One vivid example or statistic. **Do NOT**
open with a literature gap.

```
{INDUSTRY DOMAIN} routinely faces {PROBLEM}, leading to {QUANTIFIED COST /
SAFETY IMPACT}. {ONE-VIVID-EXAMPLE OR STATISTIC}. {WHY THIS MATTERS NOW —
MARKET / REGULATORY / TECHNOLOGY DRIVER}.
```

### ¶2 — Current practice & limitations

What practitioners do **today**, and why it falls short. Be specific about
the gap between aspiration and reality.

```
Current practice relies on {CURRENT METHOD}, which is {LIMITATION 1,
LIMITATION 2, LIMITATION 3}. {OPTIONAL: A SPECIFIC FAILURE CASE OR STUDY
CITING THE LIMITATION}.
```

### ¶3 — Literature gap

Move from broad to the **specific** gap this paper fills. End with the
standard gap-marker phrasing.

```
Several studies have addressed {BROAD AREA} (Author Year; Author Year).
However, {SPECIFIC GAP 1}. {SPECIFIC GAP 2}. To the best of our knowledge,
no prior work has {THE EXACT THING THIS PAPER DOES}.
```

### ¶4 — Approach preview + numbered contributions

**The most important paragraph.** The numbered list here will be answered
verbatim in Conclusion ¶ 2.

```
In this study, we propose {METHOD NAME}, a {ONE-LINE DESCRIPTION}. The
contributions of this paper are as follows:

1. We propose {METHOD NAME}, the first {PROPERTY} method for {TASK} that
   {DIFFERENTIATOR}.
2. We construct and release {DATASET}, a {PROPERTY} dataset of {SAMPLE
   COUNT} {ITEMS} for {TASK}.
3. We conduct extensive experiments demonstrating {QUANTIFIED RESULT},
   outperforming {BASELINE} by {DELTA}.
```

### ¶5 — Paper organization

Roadmap paragraph; mirrors Conclusion ¶ 1 (headline finding).

```
The remainder of this paper is organized as follows. §2 reviews related work
on {TOPIC}. §3 presents the proposed {METHOD NAME}. §4 describes the dataset
and experimental setup. §5 reports results. §6 discusses implications and
limitations. §7 concludes the paper.
```

## Self-Check (before §3.7)
- [ ] ¶4 has an explicit numbered contributions list (1, 2, 3, …).
- [ ] Each contribution is **singular and falsifiable** — not a vague claim.
- [ ] ¶1 mentions a real engineering / industry stake (cost / safety /
      downtime), not just a literature gap.
- [ ] The phrase "to the best of our knowledge" (or equivalent) appears in ¶3.

## Mirror Reminder
Every numbered contribution above MUST be answered in Conclusion ¶ 2 — in
the **same order**, with the **evidence** that supports it.
